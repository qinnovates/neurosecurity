The Gap

"Unaltered" is not the same as "self-originating"

Four neurorights have been proposed to protect your brain: Mental Privacy, Cognitive Liberty, Mental Integrity, and Psychological Continuity (Ienca & Andorno, 2017). Philosophical frameworks define what these rights mean. None specify how a BCI system would actually enforce them.

The critical gap is in Mental Integrity. Current formulations protect against modification of neural signals — tampering, injection, disruption. But we are building systems that can inject, simulate, or reshape neural signals. A perfectly intact signal that was never generated by your brain passes every integrity check and violates everything MI is supposed to protect. Governance models must distinguish between "unaltered" and "self-originating."

That distinction requires engineering, not philosophy. It requires a metric that can verify whether a signal's statistical properties match your baseline neural signature. We are unaware of a framework that had built that. So we did.

The Discovery

No one had catalogued the threats

FDA

Requires medical device cybersecurity assessments, but has no published BCI-specific attack taxonomy we are aware of. Guidance says "assess risk per ISO 14971" without defining what BCI threats look like.

MITRE

Published a medical device threat modeling playbook (2021), but it is methodology-generic. No neural-specific techniques or BCI entries in ATT&CK as of early 2026.

CISA

Created a tabletop foresight game for BCI futures planning. A board game for brainstorming, not a security framework.

BCI Manufacturers

Neuralink, Synchron, Blackrock, Precision Neuroscience: no public security documentation we are aware of. No published threat models or vulnerability disclosure policies as of early 2026.

So I built it

I catalogued every known way to attack a brain-computer interface, scored each one for neural impact, traced the affected brain regions to psychiatric outcomes, and mapped the neurorights each technique could violate. 109 techniques. 61 clinical outcome references. 4 neurorights operationalized.

The Test

Does the framework hold up?

A framework that can't be tested is a story. Here's how the atlas validates against physics, neuroscience, and simulation.

Simulation Validation

28 consumer sensor exploitation techniques modeled with synthetic signals via BrainFlow. ANC earbuds could extract ear canal biometrics at −47 dB below music. No firmware exploit. Just physics. Hardware validation pending.

Dual-Use Discovery

78 of 109 attack techniques have direct therapeutic counterparts. The same mechanism that treats Parkinson's can, if misconfigured, cause psychiatric harm.

Key Finding

The Neural Impact Chain

A six-stage research mapping that traces BCI attack techniques from their physical mechanism through the affected neural structure to their potential psychiatric outcome. 61 techniques map to symptoms matching DSM-5-TR criteria — from acute stress disorder to neurocognitive impairment. These mappings require clinical validation.

Applied Research

Project Runemate

A bytecode compiler targeting 65-90% BCI data compression (67.8% measured in simulation), making post-quantum encryption viable on low-power implantable devices. Protecting patients from unauthorized neural access requires encryption that fits within the power constraints of a brain implant. Runemate demonstrates this is feasible. Written in Rust. 200 KB on-chip.
