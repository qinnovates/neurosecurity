# Principals

**Topic:** Neuroethics — vision for a connected, safe, equitable future
**Genre:** Jazz-soul with gospel warmth (Leon Bridges x Hozier x Nina Simone — warmer than Sawdust, more hope than grief)
**BPM:** ~82
**Key:** Eb major (warm, open, rich — brighter than Sawdust's Dm)
**Production:** Rhodes piano is the foundation. Warm, not cold. Upright bass walks underneath. Brushed drums from the start (earned in Sawdust — here we begin with rhythm because the vision is already in motion). Strings enter on the bridge — not cinematic bombast, chamber quartet. Intimate. Horn section (trumpet + tenor sax) on final chorus only. Voice-first. The song breathes.
**Voices:**
- **Lead male:** Warm soul tenor (Leon Bridges warmth — intimate, conversational, like he's talking to one person in a room of thousands)
- **Female voice:** Rich alto with gospel roots (Lianne La Havas energy — grounded, wise, not belting — she IS the principle, not performing one)
- **Children's choir:** Final chorus only. 8-12 voices. The ones we live to raise. They don't harmonize perfectly. That's the point.
**Core motif:** A Rhodes chord that resolves and then opens again — never fully landing. The future isn't finished. Every resolution leads to a new question. This IS neuroethics.

## Wordplay Structure

The title operates on three levels:
1. **Principals** = the teachers, leaders, mentors who shaped us (Mr. Priddy, Mrs. Priddy, every educator who gave us tools before we knew what to build)
2. **Principles** = the ethical foundations we derived (consent, dignity, equity, accessibility, cognitive liberty)
3. **Principal** = primary, foundational — these are the principal concerns of our generation

The song never explains the wordplay. It just uses both meanings and lets the listener catch it.

## Symbolic Layer

### Three Levels of Meaning
1. **Surface:** A call for ethical technology — build it right, build it for everyone. Anyone working in tech hears a manifesto.
2. **Emotional:** The personal dream — Kevin's vision of standing at a table (UNESCO) where the world agrees that the mind is sacred. The kid who climbed the shelves now builds the framework.
3. **Hidden:** The principals (teachers) who gave us principles (values) are the reason we became principal (primary) voices for those who have none. The entire song is a thank-you letter disguised as a manifesto.

### Symbolic Vocabulary
- **The table** = UNESCO, governance bodies, the place where decisions are made. Also: the dinner table, where families connect. Also: the workbench, where things are built.
- **The room** = the mind — the last private space. Callback to Sawdust's dark room, but here it's a room we're protecting, not escaping.
- **The signal** = neural data, but also: the message, the call, what we're sending into the future.
- **The door** = access. In Sawdust we found the door by feel. Here we're holding it open for others.
- **The well** = Marcus Aurelius again (Meditations 7.59). The wellspring within — but now extended outward. If everyone has a well, no one should go thirsty.
- **The chalk** = what teachers write with. Temporary but formative. The lesson outlasts the medium.
- **The spine** = the book's spine (knowledge), the human spine (standing up), the backbone of a framework.

## Production Notes

- **Key difference from Sawdust:** Sawdust looks backward (resilience, survival, the dark room). Principals looks forward (vision, equity, the table we're building). Same artist, different direction on the timeline.
- **The unresolved chord:** The Rhodes chord that opens and closes the song never fully resolves. This is intentional — neuroethics doesn't have answers yet. The framework is a question, not a conclusion. The music should feel the same way.
- **Children's choir:** Must be real children, not processed. Imperfect harmonies. These are the ones we build for. Their imperfection IS the point — the future doesn't arrive polished.
- **No drums until sticks on final 4 bars:** Brushes throughout, sticks only at the very end. The transition from brushes to sticks = the transition from planning to action. From chalk to framework. From classroom to UNESCO.
- **The table (bridge):** This is the emotional core. The vision of a table where a village woman and a CEO have equal neural protection. This is QIF's thesis stated as a dream, not a specification.
- **Sawdust callbacks:** "The door we found in darkness / we hold open now" — direct continuation. The well within (Marcus Aurelius). The spine (bookshelf → book's spine → standing upright → framework backbone). Sawdust is the origin story. Principals is the mission statement.
