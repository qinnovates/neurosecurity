Case Study · Endogenous Threat Vectors

When Your Body Is the Threat Actor

Mapping an endogenous neural attack chain through QIF. No BCI required. Three biological threat vectors. Five attack phases. The same framework that secures brain-computer interfaces maps how the nervous system fails from the inside.

Case Study Endogenous QIF PTSD B12 COVID Neurorights Public Health

What this page is about

In 2021, three things hit at once: a vitamin B12 deficiency that had been silently damaging my nerves for years, a COVID infection that triggered neuroinflammation, and PTSD from a traumatic event that had been reshaping my brain since 2015. Each one alone was manageable. Together, they cascaded — disrupting memory, hijacking threat responses, and ultimately changing how I experienced my own identity.

As a security engineer, I realized these weren't random symptoms. They followed the same patterns I'd studied in cyberattacks on brain-computer interfaces: slow infiltration, signal jamming, replay loops, cascading failure, and persistent corruption. The difference was that no device was involved. My own biology was the attacker.

This page maps that experience using the same threat framework (QIF) I built to secure neurotechnology. It shows, step by step, how biological processes can produce the exact failure modes we worry about in BCI security — and why that matters for anyone building, regulating, or using devices that connect to the brain.

Disclaimer

This case study applies a security threat modeling framework to biological neural pathways. The author is a security engineer, not a clinician. The psychiatric mappings are research-based and require clinical validation. The neuroethical implications — which neurorights are at stake, which governance structures are needed — are the point.

The Attack Surface: Why This Is Different

QIF models threats entering through synthetic layers: firmware, drivers, signal processing, the interface band, then neural tissue. Endogenous threats skip all of that. They start inside.

The brain has always had an attack surface. BCIs just add a new port.

Dual-Vector Convergence

COVID and PTSD are independent threat actors that converge on the same neural substrate. COVID degrades bottom-up through biochemical entry at N1. PTSD exploits top-down through experiential entry at N6-N7. The weakened substrate from one vector reduces defenses against the other. Quarantine removed competing sensory input, creating the conditions for replay amplification.

The Reinforcement Loop

The chain does not terminate. Phase 4 (self-model corruption) reduces the brain's capacity to detect Phase 1 (slow drift), which accelerates degradation, which deepens the replay loop, which compounds the cascade.

Not Deterministic: The Stochastic Framing

Phase transitions are probabilities, not certainties. Not everyone exposed to Phase 1 progresses to Phase 2. Not everyone in Phase 3 reaches Phase 4. The chain describes what becomes possible at each stage, not what will happen.

Free will is the intervention point. In QIF terms: Cognitive Liberty (CL) is the neuroright that modulates transition probability at each phase boundary. The more CL is eroded (by isolation, by inflammation, by compounded damage), the higher the transition probability.

Phase Transition Probability

P(Phase N → N+1) = f(substrate_integrity,
                       environmental_stressors,
                       CRB_vulnerability,
                       cognitive_liberty_remaining,
                       free_will_intervention)  ← irreducible

Entry 78 established the determinism gradient across the hourglass: S-bands are deterministic (physics, electronics). N-bands range from stochastic (N1-N3 electrochemistry) through chaotic (N4-N5 network dynamics) to quantum uncertain (N6-N7 consciousness). The further up the hourglass, the less predictable individual outcomes become. Population-level statistics hold. Individual paths diverge.

We can't predict who will break the chain. But we can predict what degrades their probability of breaking it.

The BCI Bridge: Why This Matters for Patients

Now imagine the patients who rely on BCIs to restore their vision, or motor movements.

If endogenous threats already degrade the neural substrate, BCI-dependent patients face compounded risk. The biological degradation from Phase 1-4 weakens the same neural tissue that the BCI interfaces with. A patient whose substrate is already compromised has reduced capacity to detect or resist device-mediated attacks.

The same framework models both the biological degradation AND the device attack surface. Standard QIF threat modeling covers S3 through I0. This case study demonstrates that the N-band portion of the model also captures endogenous failure modes. Together, they represent the complete threat surface for a BCI-dependent patient living through a pandemic.

"Who or what watches the watcher to ensure trust is verified rather than inherent, given the chaotic nature of said environment(s)?"

QIF's zero-trust at I0 answers this. The interface band verifies every signal crossing the biology-silicon boundary, regardless of whether the threat originates from the device side or the biological side.

The Gap: What Doesn't Exist

No existing psychology framework chains biological and experiential threats as exploits in a kill chain with severity scoring, detection capability modeling, and defense-in-depth proposals. Here is what each framework does and what it does not do:

Framework What it does What it doesn't do
DSM-5 Classification catalog Doesn't chain how one break enables the next
Biopsychosocial Says factors interact Doesn't specify the chain
ACE scores Counts adversity No mechanism, no intervention points
Allostatic load Cumulative stress number No kill chain, no per-vector scoring
Polyvagal theory Threat response states No attack surface model
Network psychopathology Symptom nodes activate each other No severity scoring, no defense model

Psychology asks: what symptoms do you have?
Security asks: what's your attack surface, what's been exploited, what's the kill chain, and where can we insert a control?
