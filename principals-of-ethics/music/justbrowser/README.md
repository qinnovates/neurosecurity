# Auditory Learning

Music that starts with story. A way to share where I come from and express what drives this work through sound.

[Listen on Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/album/1s3McB6Rcd9LdZrKiNlH6N?si=UMRzS2qATF-e_6hkon5VgQ) · [SoundCloud](https://soundcloud.com/search?q=keviano%20kulhi)

## Why Music

The research is technical. The frameworks are abstract. But the reasons behind them are deeply personal. Music lets me encode that personal layer — the experiences, the philosophy, the fire — into something people can feel without reading a whitepaper.

Every track starts from a real story. The knowledge is woven into the lyrics, but the entry point is human experience, not curriculum.

## How It Works

1. **Start with the story** — what happened, what it meant, what it taught
2. **Thread in the knowledge** — Stoic philosophy, neuroscience concepts, security thinking
3. **Write lyrics with symbolic layers** — surface meaning anyone can feel, hidden meaning for those who dig
4. **Produce and iterate** — refine arrangement and vocal dynamics across versions
5. **Release and share** — the music carries the message further than papers can

## Sawdust (Album)

The debut project. Sawdust was an experiment using modern music to see which style gets the most listens — from pop to gospel to blues — to understand what has the most impact and reach.

**Sawdust** encodes 10 Stoic teachings from Marcus Aurelius into a call-and-response gospel blues track. Three layers of meaning: surface (building and breaking), emotional (love as the reason to build), and hidden (a personal story encoded in the symbolism).

**Principals of Ethics** is the mission statement to Sawdust's origin story — a call to action for anyone who wants to help provide equity to those who need it the most. Wordplay on principals (teachers who shaped us) and principles (ethics we derived). Encodes cognitive liberty, mental privacy, consent, and neural equity into a cinematic neo-soul gospel track that builds from a grandmother's prayer to the George Washington Bridge to a UNESCO table. References Santiago's neurorights law, UNESCO, California, and three NYC landmarks visible from Kevin's window. Full lyrics and symbolic analysis in [principals-of-ethics.md](neuroethics/principals-of-ethics.md).

## Why This Works

- **Story-first** — personal narrative creates empathy before the lesson lands
- **Emotional encoding** — music triggers emotional memory pathways, improving recall
- **Dual coding** — information in multiple modalities is retained better
- **Passive exposure** — background listening builds familiarity with concepts
- **Spaced repetition** — songs you replay naturally create spaced review
